The Department of Labor released its monthly employment report on Friday, showing that the nonfarm payrolls increased by 175,000 jobs last month, while the overall unemployment rate held steady at 3.9 percent. Federal Reserve officials stated in a press release that the current data reflects a moderating labor market, which will be taken into consideration at the upcoming interest rate policy meeting. Market analysts noted that while wage growth slowed slightly by 0.1 percent, consumer spending patterns remain resilient across major retail and manufacturing sectors."
